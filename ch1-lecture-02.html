Lecture 1: Introduction to Matrices Lecture 2: REF & RREF

1. Elementary Row Operations

To transform a matrix into Row Echelon Form (REF) or Row Reduced Echelon Form (RREF), we use three types of elementary row operations.

(i) Row Interchange

Swap two rows: \( R_i \leftrightarrow R_j \)
Example: \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \xrightarrow{R_1 \leftrightarrow R_2} \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \]

(ii) Row Scaling

Multiply a row by a nonzero constant: \( R_i \to kR_i, \, k \neq 0 \)
Example: \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \xrightarrow{R_2 \to 2R_2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 6 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \]

(iii) Row Replacement

Add a multiple of one row to another: \( R_i \to R_i + kR_j \)
Example: \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \xrightarrow{R_2 \to R_2 - 3R_1} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \]

2. Row Echelon Form (REF)

A matrix is in Row Echelon Form if:

  1. All nonzero rows are above any rows of all zeros.
  2. The leading entry of each row is to the right of the leading entry in the row above it.
  3. All entries below a leading entry are zero.

Example: Convert to REF

Consider: \[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 & -1 \\ -1 & -2 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \]
Step 1: Use \( R_1 \) as pivot row. \[ R_2 \to R_2 - 2R_1, \quad R_3 \to R_3 + R_1 \] \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \] Step 2: Eliminate below pivot in col 3. \[ R_3 \to R_3 - 2R_2 \] \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \] This is the Row Echelon Form (REF).

3. Row Reduced Echelon Form (RREF)

A matrix in REF becomes RREF if, in addition:

  • The leading entry in each nonzero row is 1.
  • Each leading 1 is the only nonzero entry in its column.

Continuing Example:

Step 3: Normalize \( R_2 \). \[ R_2 \to -\frac{1}{3}R_2 \] \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \] Step 4: Eliminate above pivot in column 3. \[ R_1 \to R_1 - R_2 \] \[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \] This is the Row Reduced Echelon Form (RREF).
