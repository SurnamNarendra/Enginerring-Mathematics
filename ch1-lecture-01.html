Definition & Theory

Introduction to Matrices is an essential concept in linear algebra and engineering mathematics. This lecture introduces definitions, notation, and theoretical background with step-by-step explanation.

Worked Examples

Example 1 (2×2 Matrix): Compute determinant of A = [[1, 2], [3, 4]].

det(A) = (1)(4) - (2)(3) = -2.

Example 2 (3×3 Matrix): Compute determinant of B = [[1, 0, 2], [2, 1, 0], [0, 3, 1]].

det(B) = 1(1*1 - 0*3) - 0(...) + 2(2*3 - 1*0) = 1 + 12 = 13.

Applications in Engineering

Introduction to Matrices is applied in analyzing electrical networks, structural engineering, computer graphics, control systems, and more.
