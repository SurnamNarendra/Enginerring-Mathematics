Lecture 7 — Introduction to Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

Topics: definition, properties, uses, examples (20 matrices), worked solutions, applications, and an interactive eigen calculator (2×2 → 4×4).

1. Definition

For a square matrix \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}\), a non-zero vector \(v\) is an eigenvector and \(\lambda\) is the corresponding eigenvalue if:

\[A v = \lambda v.\]

2. How to compute

Eigenvalues are roots of the characteristic polynomial:

\[\det(A - \lambda I) = 0.\]

Eigenvectors are non-zero solutions to \((A-\lambda I)v=0\) for each eigenvalue \(\lambda\).

Worked Examples

Example 1 — 2×2 Symmetric Matrix

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}2 & 1\\[4pt]1 & 2\end{pmatrix} \]

Step 1: Characteristic polynomial:

\[(2-\lambda)^2-1=\lambda^2-4\lambda+3=(\lambda-3)(\lambda-1)\]

Eigenvalues: 3, 1

Step 2: Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=3\): \[(A-3I)v=0 \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix}-1 & 1\\1 & -1\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}x\\y\end{pmatrix}=0 \Rightarrow x=y\] Eigenvector: \(v_1=(1,1)^T\)
For \(\lambda=1\): \[(A-I)v=0 \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix}1 & 1\\1 & 1\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}x\\y\end{pmatrix}=0 \Rightarrow x=-y\] Eigenvector: \(v_2=(1,-1)^T\)

Example 2 — 2×2 Diagonal Matrix

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}4 & 0\\[4pt]0 & -2\end{pmatrix} \]

Step 1: Characteristic polynomial:

\[(4-\lambda)(-2-\lambda)=0\]

Eigenvalues: 4, -2

Step 2: Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=4\): equation is \(x\) free, \(y=0\). Eigenvector \((1,0)^T\).
For \(\lambda=-2\): equation is \(y\) free, \(x=0\). Eigenvector \((0,1)^T\).

Example 3 — 2×2 Non-symmetric Matrix

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}0 & 1\\[4pt]-2 & -3\end{pmatrix} \]

Step 1: Characteristic polynomial:

\[\det\begin{pmatrix}-\lambda & 1\\[4pt]-2 & -3-\lambda\end{pmatrix}=\lambda^2+3\lambda+2=(\lambda+1)(\lambda+2)\]

Eigenvalues: -1, -2

Step 2: Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=-1\): \[(A+I)v=0 \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix}1 & 1\\-2 & -2\end{pmatrix}(x,y)^T=0 \Rightarrow x=-y\] Eigenvector: \((1,-1)^T\).
For \(\lambda=-2\): \[(A+2I)v=0 \Rightarrow \begin{pmatrix}2 & 1\\-2 & -1\end{pmatrix}(x,y)^T=0 \Rightarrow 2x+y=0\] Eigenvector: \((1,-2)^T\).

Example 4 — 3×3 Symmetric Matrix

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1&1\\[4pt]1&2&1\\[4pt]1&1&2\end{pmatrix} \]

Step 1: Characteristic polynomial:

\[\det(A-\lambda I)=(2-\lambda)^3+2-(2-\lambda)=(\lambda-4)(\lambda-1)^2\]

Eigenvalues: 4, 1, 1

Step 2: Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=4\): (A-4I)v=0 → x=y=z. Eigenvector: (1,1,1)^T.
For \(\lambda=1\): (A-I)v=0 → system: x+y+z=0. Two free solutions: Eigenvectors: (1,-1,0)^T, (1,0,-1)^T.

Example 5 — 3×3 Diagonal Matrix

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}5&0&0\\[4pt]0&3&0\\[4pt]0&0&-1\end{pmatrix} \]

Step 1: Characteristic polynomial:

\[(5-\lambda)(3-\lambda)(-1-\lambda)=0\]

Eigenvalues: 5, 3, -1

Step 2: Eigenvectors:

Directly standard basis vectors: (1,0,0)^T, (0,1,0)^T, (0,0,1)^T.

Example 6 — 3×3 Upper Triangular Matrix

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}1&2&3\\[4pt]0&4&5\\[4pt]0&0&6\end{pmatrix} \]

Step 1: Eigenvalues: diagonal entries → 1,4,6

Step 2: Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=1\): (A-I)v=0 → equations force x free, y=z=0. Eigenvector: (1,0,0)^T.
For \(\lambda=4\): (A-4I)v=0 → system gives -3x+2y+3z=0, 0x+0y+5z=0 → z=0, -3x+2y=0 → y=1. Eigenvector: (-2,1,0)^T.
For \(\lambda=6\): (A-6I)v=0 → equations give -5x+2y+3z=0, -2y+5z=0 → y=5, z=2y=10. Eigenvector: (-3,-5,1)^T (scaled).
