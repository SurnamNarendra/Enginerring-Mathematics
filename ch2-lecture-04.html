Lecture 4: Mean Value Theorem (MVT)

1. Introduction

The Mean Value Theorem is a fundamental theorem in calculus. It connects the average rate of change of a function over an interval to the instantaneous rate of change (derivative) at some point in that interval.

2. Statement of MVT

Let \(f(x)\) be a function that satisfies:

  • Continuous on the closed interval \([a, b]\)
  • Differentiable on the open interval \((a, b)\)
Then, there exists at least one \(c \in (a, b)\) such that: \[ f'(c) = \frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \]

3. Geometric Interpretation

The slope of the secant line connecting points \((a, f(a))\) and \((b, f(b))\) equals the slope of the tangent line at some point \(c\) in \((a,b)\).

4. Worked Example 1

Function: \(f(x)=x^2-4x+3\), Interval: [1,4]

  • Average rate of change: \(\frac{f(4)-f(1)}{4-1} = \frac{3-0}{3}=1\)
  • Derivative: \(f'(x)=2x-4\)
  • Set \(f'(c)=1 \Rightarrow 2c-4=1 \Rightarrow c=2.5\)

Hence, \(c=2.5\) satisfies MVT.

5. Worked Example 2

Function: \(f(x)=\sin x\), Interval: [0, π]

  • Average rate of change: \(\frac{\sin \pi - \sin 0}{\pi - 0} = 0\)
  • Derivative: \(f'(x)=\cos x\)
  • Set \(f'(c)=0 \Rightarrow \cos c=0 \Rightarrow c=\frac{\pi}{2}\)

MVT holds at \(c=\pi/2\).

6. Applications

  • Physics: Average velocity vs. instantaneous velocity
  • Economics: Average growth rate vs. instantaneous growth rate
  • Optimization: Estimate points where the derivative equals the average rate

7. Important Notes

  • MVT requires continuity and differentiability.
  • If \(f'(x)=0\) everywhere, \(f(x)\) is constant.
  • Rolle's Theorem is a special case of MVT where \(f(a)=f(b)\).

8. Practice Problems

  1. Verify MVT for \(f(x)=x^3-3x\) on [0,2]
  2. Find \(c\) satisfying MVT for \(f(x)=e^x\) on [0,1]
  3. Determine if MVT holds for \(f(x)=|x|\) on [-1,1]
