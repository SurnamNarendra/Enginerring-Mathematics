Assignment-2 on Matrix

A. Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

  1. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix A = [[2,1],[1,2]].
  2. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix B = [[1,2,0],[0,3,0],[0,0,4]].
  3. Find the eigenvectors corresponding to the eigenvalue 3 of C = [[1,1],[0,3]].
  4. Determine whether the matrix D = [[2,1],[1,2]] is diagonalizable and justify.
  5. Diagonalize the matrix E = [[5,4],[2,3]] and verify its eigenvalues.
  6. Diagonalize the matrix L = [[2,0,0],[0,3,4],[0,0,5]].
  7. Diagonalize the matrix M = [[1,2,0],[2,1,0],[0,0,3]].
  8. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix A = [[1,0,0],[0,2,0],[0,0,3]].
  9. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix B = [[2,1,0],[1,2,1],[0,1,2]].
  10. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix C = [[4,1,1],[1,4,1],[1,1,4]].
  11. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix D = [[0,1,0],[0,0,1],[6,-11,6]].
  12. Determine whether the matrix E = [[3,1,0],[0,3,1],[0,0,3]] is diagonalizable and find its eigenvectors.

B. Cayley-Hamilton Theorem

  1. Verify the Cayley-Hamilton theorem for the matrix G = [[1,1],[4,1]].
  2. Verify the Cayley-Hamilton theorem for the matrix H = [[0,1],[-1,0]].
  3. Verify the Cayley-Hamilton theorem for the matrix I = [[2,1,0],[0,2,1],[0,0,2]].
  4. Use the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to find the inverse of J = [[1,2],[3,4]] if it exists.

C. Quadratic Forms, Nature, and Canonical Form

  1. Find the canonical form of the quadratic form Q1(x,y) = 3x² + 4xy + y².
  2. Find the nature of Q2(x,y) = x² + 2xy + y² (positive definite, negative definite, or indefinite).
  3. Find the canonical form of Q3(x,y) = 4x² - 4xy + y².
  4. Determine the nature of Q4(x,y,z) = x² + y² - z².
  5. Determine whether Q5(x,y) = x² - y² is positive definite, negative definite, or indefinite.
  6. Find the canonical form of the quadratic form Q1(x,y,z) = x² + y² + z² + 2xy + 2xz + 2yz.
  7. Find the nature of Q2(x,y,z) = x² + 2y² + 3z² + 2xy - 2xz + 4yz (positive definite, negative definite, or indefinite).
  8. Find the canonical form of Q3(x,y,z) = 2x² - y² + z² + 2xy - 2xz + 2yz.
  9. Determine the nature of Q4(x,y,z) = x² + y² - z² + 2xy - 2yz.
  10. Determine whether Q5(x,y,z) = x² - y² + z² - 2xy + 2xz - 2yz is positive definite, negative definite, or indefinite.
