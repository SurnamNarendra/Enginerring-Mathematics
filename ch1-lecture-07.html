Lecture 7 — Introduction to Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

Topics: definition, properties, uses, examples (20 matrices), worked solutions, applications, and an interactive eigen calculator (2×2 → 4×4).

1. Definition

For a square matrix \(A\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}\), a non-zero vector \(v\) is an eigenvector and \(\lambda\) is the corresponding eigenvalue if:

\[A v = \lambda v.\]

2. How to compute

Eigenvalues are roots of the characteristic polynomial:

\[\det(A - \lambda I) = 0.\]

Eigenvectors are non-zero solutions to \((A-\lambda I)v=0\) for each eigenvalue \(\lambda\).

3. Key properties

  • There are exactly \(n\) eigenvalues counting algebraic multiplicity (complex values allowed).
  • Symmetric real matrices have real eigenvalues and an orthonormal eigenbasis.
  • \(\det(A)=\prod_i\lambda_i\) and \(\operatorname{tr}(A)=\sum_i\lambda_i\).
  • For integer \(k\ge0\), if \(Av=\lambda v\) then \(A^k v=\lambda^k v\).

4. Uses & applications

  • Stability of differential equations, modal analysis in mechanics.
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) in statistics / ML.
  • Markov chains (eigenvalue 1 and stationary distribution).
  • Quantum mechanics (Hamiltonian eigenstates).

Worked Examples of Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Example A — \(2\times2\) Symmetric Matrix

Matrix:

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}2 & 1\\[4pt]1 & 2\end{pmatrix} \]

1. Characteristic polynomial:

\[ \det(A-\lambda I)=\det\begin{pmatrix}2-\lambda & 1\\[4pt]1 & 2-\lambda\end{pmatrix}=(2-\lambda)^2-1 \] \[ (2-\lambda)^2-1=0 \;\Rightarrow\; (\lambda-3)(\lambda-1)=0 \]

Eigenvalues: \(\lambda_1=3,\;\lambda_2=1\).

2. Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=3\): \[ (A-3I)v=0 \;\Rightarrow\; v_1=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]1\end{pmatrix} \] For \(\lambda=1\): \[ (A-I)v=0 \;\Rightarrow\; v_2=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]-1\end{pmatrix} \]

Example B — \(3\times3\) Matrix

Matrix:

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1&1\\[4pt]1&2&1\\[4pt]1&1&2\end{pmatrix} \]

Note: \(A=I+J\) where \(J\) is the all-ones matrix.

Eigenvalues:

\[ \lambda=4 \quad (\text{once}), \qquad \lambda=1 \quad (\text{twice}) \]

Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=4\): \[ v_1=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]1\\[4pt]1\end{pmatrix} \] For \(\lambda=1\): \[ v_2=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]-1\\[4pt]0\end{pmatrix}, \quad v_3=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]0\\[4pt]-1\end{pmatrix} \]

Example C — \(4\times4\) Matrix

Matrix: diagonal entries \(=4\), off-diagonal entries \(=1\).

\[ A=3I+J \] where \(J\) is the \(4\times4\) all-ones matrix.

Eigenvalues:

\[ \lambda=7 \quad (\text{once}), \qquad \lambda=3 \quad (\text{thrice}) \]

Eigenvectors:

For \(\lambda=7\): \[ v_1=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]1\\[4pt]1\\[4pt]1\end{pmatrix} \] For \(\lambda=3\): a basis of orthogonal complement: \[ v_2=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]-1\\[4pt]0\\[4pt]0\end{pmatrix},\quad v_3=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]0\\[4pt]-1\\[4pt]0\end{pmatrix},\quad v_4=\begin{pmatrix}1\\[4pt]0\\[4pt]0\\[4pt]-1\end{pmatrix} \]
