Lecture-3: Row Echelon Form (REF)

1. Recap of Elementary Row Operations

To convert a matrix into Row Echelon Form (REF), we use three operations:

  • Row Interchange: Swap two rows.
  • Row Scaling: Multiply a row by a non-zero scalar.
  • Row Replacement: Replace a row by adding a multiple of another row.

2. Definition of Row Echelon Form

A matrix is in Row Echelon Form if:

  • All nonzero rows are above rows of all zeros.
  • The leading entry (pivot) of each nonzero row is strictly to the right of the leading entry of the row above it.
  • All entries below each pivot are zero.

3. Examples of Row Echelon Form

Example 1:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 2:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 3:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\ 0 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 4:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 2 \\ 0 & 3 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 5:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 4 & 6 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 6:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 5 & 4 \\ 0 & 0 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 7:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 7 & 3 & 2 \\ 0 & 0 & 5 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 8:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 9:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 4 & 6 \\ 0 & 3 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \]
Example 10:
\[ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 & 5 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]
