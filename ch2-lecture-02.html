Lecture 2: Functions of One Variable — Limits and Continuity

1. Concept of a Limit

Limits describe the behavior of a function as the input approaches some value. Symbolically,

\( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \)

means that as \( x \) gets arbitrarily close to \( a \), the function values get arbitrarily close to \( L \).

Example: \( \lim_{x \to 2} (3x+1) = 7 \).

2. Left-hand and Right-hand Limits

  • Left-hand limit: \( \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) \) → approach from left.
  • Right-hand limit: \( \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) \) → approach from right.

The limit exists only if both one-sided limits exist and are equal.

Example: For \( f(x)=\frac{|x|}{x} \),
  • \( \lim_{x \to 0^-} f(x) = -1 \)
  • \( \lim_{x \to 0^+} f(x) = 1 \)
Since left ≠ right, \( \lim_{x \to 0} f(x) \) does not exist.

3. Properties of Limits

  • \( \lim (f+g) = \lim f + \lim g \)
  • \( \lim (f \cdot g) = (\lim f)(\lim g) \)
  • \( \lim \frac{f}{g} = \frac{\lim f}{\lim g}, \; \lim g \neq 0 \)
Example: \( \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2-1}{x-1} = \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x-1} = \lim_{x \to 1} (x+1) = 2 \).

4. Infinite Limits & Limits at Infinity

\( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty \) means values grow without bound.

\( \lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) \) means behavior for very large \( x \).

Example: \( \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0 \).

5. Continuity

A function \( f \) is continuous at \( x=a \) if:

  1. \( f(a) \) is defined,
  2. \( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) \) exists,
  3. \( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a) \).
Example: \( f(x)=x^2 \) is continuous for all \( x \in \mathbb{R} \).

6. Types of Discontinuity

  • Removable: limit exists but function value is different/undefined.
  • Jump: left-hand and right-hand limits exist but are unequal.
  • Infinite: function tends to \( \pm \infty \) near a point.
Example: \( f(x)=\frac{x^2-1}{x-1} \) has removable discontinuity at \( x=1 \).

7. Applications of Limits and Continuity

  • Physics: Instantaneous velocity as a limit of average velocity.
  • Economics: Marginal cost as a limit of change in cost per unit.
  • Engineering: Stress-strain curves are continuous functions.

8. Practice Problems

  1. Find \( \lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2-9}{x-3} \).
  2. Does \( \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{|x|}{x} \) exist?
  3. Classify the type of discontinuity of \( f(x)=\tan x \) at \( x=\pi/2 \).
  4. Check if \( f(x)=\begin{cases} x^2 & x\neq 2 \\ 5 & x=2 \end{cases} \) is continuous at \( x=2 \).
  5. Evaluate \( \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2+2}{5x^2+7} \).
