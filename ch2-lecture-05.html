Lecture 5: Indeterminate Forms & L’Hospital’s Rule

1. Introduction

In calculus, while finding limits, sometimes direct substitution results in forms like \(\frac{0}{0}\) or \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\). These are called indeterminate forms. L’Hospital’s Rule is a technique to resolve these forms using derivatives.

2. Indeterminate Forms

Common indeterminate forms include:

  • \(\frac{0}{0}\)
  • \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\)
  • \(0 \cdot \infty\)
  • \(\infty - \infty\)
  • \(0^0, 1^\infty, \infty^0\)

3. L’Hospital’s Rule

If \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) / g(x)\) results in \(\frac{0}{0}\) or \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\), then: \[ \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} \] provided the right-hand limit exists.

4. Worked Examples

Example 1: Simple Polynomial

Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2-4}{x-2}\)

  • Direct substitution: \(\frac{0}{0}\) → indeterminate
  • Apply L’Hospital’s Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^2-4)/\frac{d}{dx}(x-2) = \frac{2x}{1}\)
  • Evaluate limit: \(2*2 = 4\)

Example 2: Trigonometric Function

Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x}\)

  • Direct substitution: \(\frac{0}{0}\)
  • Derivative: \(f'(x)/g'(x) = \cos x / 1\)
  • Limit: \(\cos 0 = 1\)

Example 3: Exponential Function

Evaluate \(\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{e^x}{x^2}\)

  • Direct substitution: \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\)
  • Derivative: \(\frac{d}{dx}(e^x)/\frac{d}{dx}(x^2) = e^x / 2x\)
  • Apply L’Hospital again: \(e^x / 2x \to \infty\)
  • Limit: \(\infty\)

5. Worked Example with Graph

Function: \(f(x) = \frac{x^2-1}{x-1}\), Limit as \(x \to 1\)

  • Substitute: \(\frac{0}{0}\)
  • Derivative: \(f'(x) = \frac{2x}{1} = 2x\)
  • Limit: \(2*1 = 2\)

6. Special Forms & Tips

  • For \(0\cdot\infty\), convert to \(\frac{0}{0}\) or \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\) by algebra.
  • For \(\infty-\infty\), combine terms to form a fraction.
  • Exponential indeterminate forms \(1^\infty, 0^0, \infty^0\) → use logarithms.

7. Real-Life Applications

  • Physics: Calculating instantaneous velocity from average velocity
  • Economics: Instantaneous rate of growth of profit/cost
  • Engineering: Limits in control systems or signal processing
